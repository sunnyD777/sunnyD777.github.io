Introduction

The planet Earth faces many major problems. In a micro perspective such problems include racism, inequality, and sexism, and while these problems are very troublesome for the inhabitants of Earth, they do not affect the planet itself. Looking in a macro perspective, the greatest problem that both the Earth and Earthlings face is Climate Change, with the most major consquence of it being the rise in sea levels.


Why Is It Happening?

  • Thermal Expansion

    • As ocean water heats up, its density decreases. Considering mass must remain constant and that Density = Mass/Volume, volume must increase as it is inversely proportional with density. This is what is known as thermal expansion. Water expands proportional to a given heat input, so the global average expansion of ocean water depends on the ubiquitous distribution of heat within the ocean("11.2 Factors"). Over the past century, the average global temperature within oceans has risen 0.18°F ("Sea Temperature Rise.")






  • Melting of Glaciers & Ice Caps

    • Another reason why the sea level is increasing is due to the increased melting of large ice formations such as glaciers and ice caps. Typically in the summer, these ice formations become partially melted due to the temperature of the season, which is then later balanced out in winter when it snows. Recently, however, due to the increasing rise in the global average temperature, there have been greater than average summer meltings coupled with less snowing due to later winters and earlier springs. Because of this imbalance, the ratio between runoff meltwater and ocean evaporation has increased significantly thus causing the sea level to rise ("Sea Level Rise.").



What Are the Consequences?

Increased sea level elevation can have catastrophic effects on coastal habitats. Rising sea levels can send salt water further inland where it can contaminate fresh groundwater and ruin peoples' fresh water supply. Additionally, higher sea levels give rise to higher and more destructive waves which can result in destructive coastal erosion. During storms, this erosion can become even more cataclysmic and ruin important things such soil, roads, and infrastructures. And most obviously, increased sea levels can lead to increased vulnerability to major flooding and could potentially force people to abandon their homes. The flooding may even completely submerge small island nations. Almost half of the world's population, 3 billion people, live within 200 km of a coastline, and are thus vulnerable to such effects ("Sea Levels Are").









How to Prevent It?

The root cause of sea level elevation is global warming. The atmospheric increase in gaseous compounds that trap heat, otherwise known as greenhouse gases, has caused the Earth's temperatures to rise, with oceans absorbing around 80% of this additional heat ("Sea Level Rise"). Many of these greenhouse emissions are caused by human activity, and to counter future sea level rise, we must implement the following.

  • Renewable Energy Source

    • The burning of fossil fuels has become an integral and essential part of our society. One key example of how we use it is for the fuel for our cars. Unfortunately when fossil fuels are burnt, the carbon inside of them is released and combines with oxygen in the air to yield carbon dioxide(CO2), one of the most major greenhouse gases. Additionally as the name suggests, fossil fuels are formed from prehistoric fossils and are considered a non-renewable source of energy because they take millions of years to form. A better and more efficient form of energy would be renewable energy, energy formed from natural resources that are continuously replenished (i.e. sunlight, wind, rain, etc.). Use of renewable energy sources would alleviate carbon emission into the atmosphere, and effectively thus help reduce the rate of global warming and seal level rise ("Causes of the").






  • Less Deforestation

    • Another contributor to the global warming is deforestation, the cutting down of trees and forests for industrial use. Trees' importance lies in their ability to perform the process known as photosynthesis, where plants use energy from sunlight to synthesize food. In this process, trees intake carbon dioxide and release oxygen, so increased deforestation not only leads to increased concentration CO2 in the atmosphere, it also gives us less oxygen to breathe. Additionally the burning of tree bark releases the stored carbon back into the air where it once again turns into carbon dioxide. Instead of destroying trees for large scale developments, we should rather start planting them to improve the livelihood of our planet ("Causes of the").
